# Bio
Psychologist & Neuroscience Researcher
Emilia Gyr is a psychologist and early-career researcher specializing in the study of emotions, social interaction, and their neurobiological foundations. She holds a Master’s degree in Psychoanalytical Clinical Intervention from the Pontifical Catholic University of Peru, where she trained in Interpersonal Dynamical Therapy and gained extensive experience in clinical assessment, intervention, and research.
Her work focuses on understanding intra- and interpersonal processes through a multidisciplinary lens that integrates psychotherapy, neuroscience, and experimental psychology.
Alongside her research, Emilia works clinically with adult patients, supporting individuals experiencing depression, anxiety, and personality disorders. Her therapeutic approach emphasizes the quality of the patient–therapist relationship as a central mechanism of change, integrating clinical sensitivity with scientific insight.
